# Laser Cutting

***

---

___

This next project was focused on 2D fabrication using a laser cutter. The main goal was to take an idea from a 3D design, successfully translate it into 2D cutting paths, and create a physical object. I chose to make a simple, slot-together Christmas tree out of cardboard. This process involved modeling the object in CAD, exporting the flat patterns as DXF files, and finally, operating the laser cutter to fabricate the parts.

## 3D Prototype

***

---

___

For this project, I wanted to design and build a small object using the laser cutter. I decided on a slotted Christmas tree made from cardboard, which would be easy to assemble. The entire process started in CAD software, where I designed a complete 3D prototype to make sure everything would fit together.

### DXF files

***

---

___

Once the 3D models were finalized, I had to translate them into a 2D format for the laser cutter. I laid each component flat and exported its outline as a DXF file. These files are simple 2D vector drawings that tell the laser exactly where to cut.

## Cutting Process

***

---

___

This was the hands-on part. I took my DXF files and uploaded them to the laser cutter's software. I used a sheet of cardboard for this prototype. I had to dial in the correct power and speed settings on the laser to ensure it would cut cleanly through the cardboard without starting a fire. After a quick test, I ran the job and the machine cut out all the pieces in just a minute or two.
